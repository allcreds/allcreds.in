Understanding Debt Collection

Debt collection is a critical function for financial institutions and businesses to recover outstanding dues while maintaining customer trust. A professional and ethical approach is essential to ensure compliance and preserve long-term relationships.

Key Elements of Ethical Collection

  • Regulatory Compliance: Following RBI and local legal guidelines.
  • Customer Communication: Transparent, respectful, and solution-oriented outreach.
  • Timely Follow-ups: Using automated reminders and field agents when necessary.
  • Dispute Resolution: Mechanisms to handle customer concerns fairly.

At Allcreds, we implement data-driven collection strategies combined with human-centric communication to recover dues efficiently and ethically.

In-Depth Debt Collection Strategies

  • Target Audience Sensitivity: NBFCs and Microfinance institutions serve low-income populations. Collection strategies must be empathetic and respectful.
  • Field-Based Recovery: Field agents visit borrowers personally, especially in rural areas, to build trust and remind or collect dues.
  • Digital & Automated Reminders: SMS, WhatsApp, and IVR are used for efficient EMI reminders and reduced manual intervention.
  • Telecalling Support: Soft calls from trained BPO agents help manage early delinquencies and confirm payments proactively.
  • Compliance with Guidelines: All recovery steps strictly follow RBI norms and ensure ethical, non-coercive practices.
  • Loan Portfolio Monitoring: Real-time tracking and analytics tools help identify risky accounts and optimize collection strategies.
  • Settlement & Restructuring Support: Flexible repayment and settlement options are provided based on borrower circumstances.
